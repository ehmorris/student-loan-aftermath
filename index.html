How will student loans effect you? An overview for Northeastern students

1. The Costs

Over the course of five years, costs go up at almost every school — here's Northeastern's tuition over the last five years, for each academic year (2 semesters.)

  1. 2009–2010 tuition: $35,712

  2. 2010–2011 tuition: $37,142

  3. 2011–2012 tuition: $38,602

  4. 2012–2013 tuition: $40,086

  5. 2013–2014 tuition: $41,660

That's $14,642 in tuition increases.

Room and Board not included, which can be as much as $13,000 per year, but varies depending on placement.

* Northeastern tuition data

2. The Debt

48% of the student body at Northeastern has taken on federal loans, at an average of $7,324 per academic year. This leaves the average Northeastern borrower with about the average national level of $29,400 in loans after graduation.
